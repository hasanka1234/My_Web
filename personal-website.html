Smart Helmet for Industrial Workplaces

IoT Sensor Integration GPS Embedded Systems

The Smart Safety Helmet is a wearable device designed to improve safety in industrial environments. It features fall detection, gas sensing, GPS tracking, and emergency alerts, enabling real-time response. An SOS and cancel button give users quick, manual control in emergencies.

View Details

Project Goals

Reduce workplace accidents, improve emergency response, and create a safer working environment using smart technology. It combines real-time monitoring, environmental sensing, and wireless communication to support industrial safety standards.


Technologies Used

The system uses technologies such as an ESP32 microcontroller, sensors for gas, pressure, vibration, and motion, and modules for GPS and GSM communication. All components are integrated into a compact, rechargeable helmet unit.


Current Status

Ongoing project (March 2025 - Present) in development phase with working prototypes demonstrating core safety features.

Water Management System for Low-Sloped Streams

Solar Energy Pump Systems Renewable Energy

This project focuses on creating sustainable, solar-powered water management systems for agricultural irrigation in remote areas. It aims to ensure efficient water use, reduce energy costs, and support reliable farming practices where access to electricity and infrastructure is limited.

View Details

Project Goals

Water Management System for Low Sloped Water Streams, aims to solve the issue of water scarcity in farming areas where water levels are low and electricity access is limited. To address this, we propose using solar-powered pumps to provide a sustainable water supply.


Technologies Used

The project uses solar energy systems to power water pumps, removing the need for electricity or fuel. Different types of pump technologies are tested to find the most efficient options for low-flow conditions.


Current Status

Ongoing project (August 2024 - Present) with promising initial results from feasibility studies.

Portable Driving Assistant & Crash Notification System

Arduino Sensors GPS GSM

Low-cost, portable Advanced Driver Assistance System for vehicles in low-income regions with crash detection and emergency notifications.


The project was later recognized as the best at the university.

View Details

Project Goals

To develop an affordable, portable driver assistance system that could improve road safety in regions where advanced vehicle safety features are not commonly available.

Technologies Used

Arduino microcontrollers, ultrasonic sensors for proximity detection, vibration sensors for crash detection, GPS for location tracking, GSM modules for emergency notifications.

Challenges & Solutions

Creating a system that was both portable and reliable enough for vehicular use. Addressed this by designing a modular system with robust power management and secure mounting options.

Results

Successfully developed a working prototype that demonstrates the feasibility of low-cost driver assistance technology for vehicles without built-in safety systems.
